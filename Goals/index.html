CLEAN WATER AND SANITATION

Sustainable management of water resources and access to safe water and sanitation are essential for unlocking economic growth and productivity, and provide significant leverage for existing investments in health and education.

Today, 2.2 billion people lack access to safely managed drinking water, and more than 4.2 billion people lack safely managed sanitation. Climate change is exacerbating the situation, with increasing disasters such as floods and droughts. 80 per cent of wastewater in the world flows back into the ecosystem without being treated or reused, and 70 per cent of the world’s natural wetland extent has been lost, including a significant loss of freshwater species.
Source : SDGS

AFFORDABLE AND CLEAN ENERGY

Our environment provides a series of renewable and non-renewable energy. Increased use of non-renewable energy without actions to mitigate greenhouse gases will have global climate change implications.

Today, 3 billion people rely on wood, coal, charcoal, and animal waste to cook. Energy, especially the non-renewable one is the dominant contributor to climate change. Since 1990, global emissions of CO2 have increased by more than 46 per cent.
Source : SDGS

CLIMATE
ACTION

Our climate is heating rapidly. The average global temperatures have increased by 1.2 degrees Celsius since 1880, particularly in the late 20th century. According to IPCC, warming beyond 1.5 degrees could have irreversible and irreparable consequences.

There's alot of way to fix this. One of them is to spread the word, to let everybody know that our planet is in danger from the climate change. One of the real action to fight this is to use clean and renewable energy. That will help our planet alot.
Source : SDGS

LIFE BELOW
WATER

Did you know that 1 in 5 fish caught comes from illegal and unregulated fishing. And did you also know that there is 17 million metric tons of plastic pollution in 2021, imagine all the plastic pollution in the ocean that is quickly adding up, harming the planet's largest ecosystem, the ocean.

Over three billion people depend on marine and coastal resources for their livelihoods. In addition, oceans are crucial for global food security and human health. If we kept polluting the ocean and using unethical way to fish, all of us can't survive on this planet.
Source : SDGS

LIFE ON
LAND

A Fundamental shift in humanity's, relationship with nature is essential. Forests have a significant role in reducing the risk of natural disasters, including floods, droughts, landslides and other extreme events. It's not only forest, there's also mountains with it's water supply and ecosystem.

The world is currently facing the largest species extinction. 100 million hectares of land was degraded every year. Let's all protect our ecosystem, and be the heroes of our planet. We can start by cleaning your local parks and forests, protect all species from extinction, and prevent deforestation.
Source : SDGS
