Planet Earth and Plastic


The use of plastic in Indonesia and the world, and how the government formulates waste management policies that are guided by 3R.


Global plastic production has exceeded levels before the Covid-19 pandemic.

• The largest contributors to global plastic production are China (32%), North America (18%), and other Asian countries (17%).
• Based on plastic type, bio-based plastic production grew significantly (51.28%) compared to fossil-based (3.62%) and recycled (2.85%).
Source : Plastic Europe


Plastic is one of the business sectors that makes a large contribution to manufacturing output in Indonesia. In the last 10 years, plastic production output ranked fifth place (5,37%) after basic metals, motorbikes, chemicals and food. Most of the plastic consumption in Indonesia is used for packaging, while the use of plastic for automotive and construction is relatively low.
The use of plastic for packaging in Indonesia is relatively higher than the use of plastic for packaging globally (31.26%).
Source : OECD


The Indonesian government, through the Ministry of Environment and Forestry (KLHK), has developed a waste management policy direction that is guided by reuse, reduce and recycle.

• This framework is contained in Law Number 18 of 2008 concerning Waste Management and Government Regulation Number 81 of 2012 concerning Management of Household Waste.
• To implement this, local governments are given the authority to determine waste management policies and strategies


There's 5 way to reduce plastic waste.
• Reduce the use and production of plastic.
• Improve waste management.
• Reduce sources of solid waste.
• Public and law enforcement involvement.
• Redesign and innovation.
